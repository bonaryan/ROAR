Resiliency Recommendation Details

More details on each resiliency opportunity identified

Increase Cooling Setpoints

Your building starts cooling at lower temperature than typical. Check the occupied and unoccupied cooling setpoint during the cooling season. Cooling system and auxiliaries’ energy consumption will be reduced by increasing the cooling setpoint

Reduce Equipment Schedules

Your building equipment load is higher than typical. Equipment and systems within any building should operate using a schedule. Check equipment schedule and if equipment is operational during low occupancy times or during reduced building use. Setup a notification to identify when schedules are overridden and are not returned to normal.

Add/Fix Economizers

Utilizing outside air that is cooler and/or drier than indoor air in an economizer can significantly reduce the energy used to cool the building. Check existing economizers, if any, for efficient operations.

Increase Heating System Efficiency

Your building heating load is higher than similar buildings for similar weather conditions. HVAC system performance has big impact on building energy consumption. Check the heating system, related equipment and controls for efficient operations. Upgrading your system to a more efficient model, will reduce your system energy consumption.


Understand the Model

Baseload

Energy consumption of all non-weather-related equipment like computers and lighting. The lower the baseload, the less the energy consumed in plugs and permanently plugged equipment.

Cooling Sensitivity

Cooling system energy consumption for each degree increase in outdoor temperature. Low cooling sensitivity results in less energy-consuming cooling system.

Cooling change-point

The temperature at which cooling system starts. Below the cooling change point, the cooling system is not operational.

Heating Sensitivity

Heating system consumption of energy for each degree decrease in outdoor temperature. Low heating sensitivity results in less energy-consuming heating system.

Heating change-point

The temperature at which heating system starts. Above the heating change point, the cooling system is not operational.


What is ROAR?

The Resiliency Opportunity Assessment + Response (ROAR) helps building owners and managers quickly assess potential risks from climate change and potential opportunities for improved resiliency, to inform decisions on where to set prioritized resiliency efforts. The tool can identify low and no-cost opportunities that can be implemented immediately, as well as retrofit opportunities that can be investigated further through more detailed audits or studies.

The tool uses different methodologies to assess different types of risks: (1) Stochastic process for stress risk module; (2) Extreme Value analysis for shock risk module; and (3) Monte-Carlo regression model for energy risk module. Those methods are applied to analyze a building component's data and weather, in order to determine how much probability of failure is expected for its respective function and the consequence(s) that arise as a result.

The performance of the system and component are then aggregated to obtain the total climate value at risk of the building. In addition to telling you whether a system or component's risk level is high, moderate or low, it goes a step further to tell you why that is the case. If a risk is categorized as high, for example, it can tell you it is because the asset is highly sensitive to the extreme weather, poorly located, or critical in terms of function and monetary value while the no climate change scenario acts as the baseline. With this information, a building owner can take the necessary actions, for example, to adjust the operation scheme of the asset, add protective barriers, do maintenance check and retrofit, perform an engineering audit that focuses on specific asset, or transfer the risk with insurance. As such, building owners and managers have a sound basis to strategically invest in effective measures to enhance resilience and maintain property valuations.
