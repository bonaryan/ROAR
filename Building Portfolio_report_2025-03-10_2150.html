Risk Levels

The chart below shows the risk levels and savings potentials for each component in your building. The X-axis represents consequence level, and the Y-axis represents occurence probability level. If your building is dominated by extreme-shock events where high-consequences low-probability risk poses, it will sit somewhere at the bottom of the chart, since occurence probability will be somewhere below the quadran. Meanwhile, high-probability low-consequence risk sits somewhere at the upper left of the quadran. To reduce bias of the potentially same level of risk from these two risk categories, scatter bubble is introduced. Bubble size indicates the potential cost savings in Canada dollars ($) – the bigger the bubble, the higher that building’s savings potential, and hence indicate prioritization for treatment. Hover your cursor over each bubble to display key stats about each component, including its ID from the Building input spreadsheet, its risk type, its capital cost, its potential cost savings, and its potential cost savings percentage.
